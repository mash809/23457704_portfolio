I identified five weak or vulnerable security implementations: 
  1. Reused passwords across multiple accounts, 
  2. Default router administrator credentials, 
  3. Outdated software with no recent patching,
  4. Unsecured public Wi-Fi access without proper verification, and
  5. Weak physical locks on low-value storage areas.  

These examples show that poor security often comes from convenience, neglect, or lack of awareness rather than advanced attack methods.
