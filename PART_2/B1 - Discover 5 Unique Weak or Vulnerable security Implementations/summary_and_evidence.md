I identified five weak or vulnerable security implementations: 
  
  1. Reused passwords across multiple accounts (https://www.cyber.gov.au/about-us/view-all-content/reports-and-statistics/annual-cyber-threat-report-2023-2024)

  2. Default router usernames and passwords (https://www.cyber.gov.au/protect-yourself/staying-secure-online/secure-your-wifi-and-router)
   
  3. No multi-factor authentication(https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa22-137a)
   
  4. Weak credentials or poor access controls on edge devices (https://www.cyber.gov.au/business-government/protecting-devices-systems/hardening-systems-applications/network-hardening/securing-edge-devices/mitigation-strategies-for-edge-devices-practitioner-guidance)
     
  5. Incorrect privileges or access control list errors (https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa22-137a)

These examples show that poor security often comes from convenience, neglect, or lack of awareness rather than advanced attack methods.
