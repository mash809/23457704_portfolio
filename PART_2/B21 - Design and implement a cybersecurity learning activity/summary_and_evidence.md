I designed and implemented a cybersecurity learning activity called Understanding Why Strong Passwords Matter. The activity was created to help students understand how weak passwords can be guessed easily and why stronger passwords are important for protecting accounts and personal information. It asks students to test several sample passwords using a password strength checker, compare the results, and reflect on what makes a password weak or strong.

The activity uses examples such as password123 and student2026 as weak passwords, and a longer password such as BlueTiger!Runs47Cloud as a strong example. Students compare estimated cracking times and identify why short and predictable passwords are easier to break, while longer and more complex passwords are safer. The activity also includes an important reminder not to test real passwords, only fake sample ones.

Overall, this learning activity is practical and easy to understand because it lets students see the difference between weak and strong passwords directly. Its learning outcome is that students understand that password length and unpredictability are more important than simply adding one number or symbol, and that stronger passwords reduce the risk of brute force attacks and unauthorised access.
