Cybersecurity Learning Activity

Understanding why strong passwords matter and how weak passwords can be guessed through brute force attacks.

Activity Title

Understanding Why Strong Passwords Matter

Objective

The aim of this activity is to help students understand how weak passwords can be guessed easily and why strong passwords are important for protecting accounts and personal information.

Activity Description

In this activity, students test different sample passwords and compare how strong or weak they are. The activity shows how simple passwords, such as names, birthdays, or common words, can be guessed quickly, while longer passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols are much harder to break.

Materials Needed

  • A computer
  • Internet access
  • A password strength checker website
  • Examples: Bitwarden Password Strength Tester or Security.org Password Checker

Steps

  1. Create five example passwords.
  2. Include weak passwords such as password123 or student2026.
  3. Include stronger passwords such as a long passphrase with random words, numbers, and symbols.
  4. Enter each sample password into a password strength checker.
  5. Record how long the tool estimates it would take to crack each password.
  6. Compare the results and identify what made some passwords weak and others strong.
  7. Write a short reflection explaining what was learned.
Important: Do not test your real password. Only use fake sample passwords for this activity.

Example Password Comparison

Password Example Strength Reason
password123 Weak Common and easy to guess.
student2026 Weak Uses a common word and predictable year.
BlueTiger!Runs47Cloud Strong Longer, harder to guess, and uses different character types.

Example Result

A password like password123 may be cracked very quickly because it is common and predictable. A password like BlueTiger!Runs47Cloud is much stronger because it is longer and harder to guess.

Learning Outcome

By completing this activity, students learn that password length and unpredictability are more important than simply adding one number or symbol. They also learn that strong passwords reduce the risk of brute force attacks and unauthorised access.

Reflection

This activity helped me understand that weak passwords are one of the easiest ways for attackers to access accounts. I learned that using personal information or common words makes a password unsafe. A strong password should be long, unique, and difficult to guess. This activity also showed me why using different passwords for different accounts and enabling two-factor authentication is important.
