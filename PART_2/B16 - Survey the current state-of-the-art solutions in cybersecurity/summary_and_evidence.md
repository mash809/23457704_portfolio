I scoped the topic to modern enterprise defensive cybersecurity solutions, rather than general company products. I defined state of the art as solutions that are consistently supported by credible standards, official guidance, and reproducible research results, not just vendor claims. Under that definition, current state-of-the-art cybersecurity focuses on approaches that improve prevention, detection, response, and access control in modern distributed environments.

Based on this scope, I found that the strongest evidence-supported state-of-the-art areas include Zero Trust Architecture, artificial intelligence and machine learning for intrusion and threat detection, and automated detection and response platforms such as Endpoint Detection and Response and Extended Detection and Response. Zero Trust Architecture is strongly supported by NIST as a modern security model that moves away from perimeter-based trust and instead continuously verifies users, devices, and resources.
https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/SpecialPublications/NIST.SP.800-207.pdf

In addition, recent review literature shows that artificial intelligence and machine learning are widely used in modern cybersecurity for intrusion detection, malware classification, behavioural analysis, and threat intelligence. These methods are considered state of the art because they are repeatedly evaluated in research using measurable performance such as detection accuracy, precision, recall, and false positive rates.
https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10115-025-02429-y

I also found that automated detection and response has become a major modern direction in practice, because organisations need faster response to threats across endpoints, networks, cloud systems, and user accounts. The practical application of these solutions is that they help security teams reduce manual workload, detect suspicious behaviour earlier, and respond more quickly to attacks. Overall, my survey showed that state-of-the-art cybersecurity is best understood not as a single tool or product, but as an evidence-based combination of zero trust, intelligent detection, and automated response.
https://www.cisa.gov/topics/cybersecurity-best-practices
