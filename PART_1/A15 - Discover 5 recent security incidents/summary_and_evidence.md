I researched five recent security incidents affecting organisations and critical systems. These included 

1. Active exploitation of Cisco SD-WAN appliances
   - Threat actors were actively exploiting Cisco SD-WAN vulnerabilities, including CVE-2026-20127, to add rogue peers, escalate privileges, and gain root access. This was treated as a critical threat because compromised SD-WAN infrastructure can give attackers long-term access to important networks.

2. INC Ransom affiliate campaign targeting critical networks
   - INC Ransom affiliates were observed targeting Australian healthcare entities using compromised accounts. In some cases, they escalated privileges, moved laterally through networks, and exfiltrated sensitive personal and medical information. 

3. Widespread BADCANDY device compromises in Australia
   - ASD assessed that more than 400 devices in Australia were potentially compromised with BADCANDY from July 2025, with more than 150 still compromised in late October 2025. This incident highlighted the risk of unpatched network devices remaining exposed for long periods.

4. Play ransomware activity affecting hundreds of entities
   - The Play ransomware group continued to be a major threat, with updated joint guidance issued in June 2025. The advisory noted current tactics, techniques, and indicators of compromise based on investigations as recent as January 2025. 

5. The targeting of Cisco ASA 5500-X series vulnerabilities in Australia.
   - ASD’s ACSC warned that multiple vulnerabilities affecting Cisco ASA 5500-X series devices were being targeted within Australia. This showed that firewall and edge devices remained attractive targets for attackers seeking access into organisational networks.
  
  
These incidents show that ransomware, exploited vulnerabilities, and compromised network devices remain major current threats.
