I participated in an online cybersecurity discussion by responding to a Reddit post from a cybersecurity student who felt discouraged after falling for a scam. In my comment, I explained that falling for a scam does not mean someone is bad at cybersecurity, because scammers are skilled at manipulating people and even careful individuals can be caught. I added that what matters most is learning from the experience and improving awareness for the future. This discussion was relevant to cybersecurity because it focused on scams, human error, and the importance of awareness and learning in cyber defence.
