Using the article 14 Types of Locks: Security, Vulnerabilities, & Best Uses, I identified ten different types of locks and noted their common uses.

1. Padlock – portable lock commonly used for gates, lockers, and storage.

2. Deadbolt – strong door lock often used on external residential doors.

3. Smart and electronic lock – modern lock using keypads, apps, or biometrics.

4. High-security lock – advanced lock designed to resist picking, drilling, and forced entry.

5. Door knob lock – common lock built into the door knob, usually used with another lock.

6. Lever handle lock – easy-to-use lock often found in offices and public buildings.

7. Cam lock – small lock commonly used on cabinets, drawers, and mailboxes.

8. Mortise lock – lock fitted inside a door, often used in commercial and stronger residential doors.

9. Rim lock – surface-mounted lock often used on older or traditional doors.

10. Euro profile cylinder – common cylinder lock used in many doors, especially in Europe.

These examples show that locks vary depending on security level, convenience, and where they are installed.
