I researched two hallucination cases involving generative AI. 

1. Microsoft Copilot falsely claimed that a German journalist had confessed to a serious crime and described him using fabricated criminal details. These false claims were hallucinations based on court cases the journalist had previously worked on. 

2. A Victorian mayor was falsely described by ChatGPT as having been charged with serious criminal offences, even though he was actually a whistleblower. These examples show how generative AI can produce confident but entirely false information that may be harmful if accepted without verification.
